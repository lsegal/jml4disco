Milestone 4

Profiling Results

The following is the results from running TPTP on the various versions of the distributed projects. Profiling was done mostly to attempt to discover why the Milestone 3 prototype ran in only 4 seconds (mysteriously). Three versions were profiled: the original prototype, the Milestone 3 prototype and a newly designed Milestone 4 version. The profiling tests were run on two separate machines.

How to interpret

The most important thing to look at from this profiling data is the number of calls made to the various components. Since the TPTP profiler dramatically slows down the timing of the program being profiled, the timing results are useful only in a ratio measure of how long each component to relative to the others.

Results

The most striking feature of the Milestone 3 prototype versus the other two versions is that it makes significantly fewer calls to all three provers (in the case of Isabelle it is making an average of 200 calls to the Isabelle adapter while the other two versions average about 1500 calls). This indicates that the ridiculous speed increase that was generated by the Milestone 3 prototype was mostly likely caused by the omission of certain VC's
